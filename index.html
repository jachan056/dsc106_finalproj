A Tale of Extremes

While the global average tells one story, precipitation is intensely local. The Earth's water distribution creates stark contrasts that define ecological survival.

The Amazon Rainforest

A critical global carbon sink heavily reliant on consistent, massive daily rainfall year-round.

The Sahara Desert

An arid extreme where evaporation vastly outpaces historical precipitation, leaving the landscape barren.

Mawsynram, India

Recognized as the wettest place on Earth, experiencing unprecedented and concentrated seasonal rainfall spikes.
